Dialogue Writing

 A Dialogue means "conversation between two people". When you tend to write a dialogue you have to imagine two persons. You have to think about both sides of the character.

Procedure

  • 1. Carefully think over the topic given to you and write the arguments or opinions of the imaginary characters briefly
  • 2. Arrange your thoughts in a logical sequence
  • 3. Imagine what would be how each character in the conversation would express his perspectives
  • 4. Make your imaginary characters talk in an easy and natural manner
  • 5. Keep in mind how real people talk in friendly conversation and use that style as you can
  • 6. When your dialogue is completed it should read like a real, spontaneous conversation

Example

Diana: Hi, how are you doing?

Elena: I'm Good. How about you?

Diana: I'm pretty good. Thanks for asking.

Elena: It's Okay. So how have you been?

Diana: I've been great. What about you?

Elena: I've been good. I'm in college right now.

Diana: What college do you go to?

Elena: I go to NN College Of Arts

Diana: Good luck with your college.

Elena: Thank you very much.
