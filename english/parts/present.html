Present Tense And It's Types

When an action takes place in the present time then the Tense is said to be Present Tense

There are four types in Present Tense

  1. Simple Present Tense
  2. Present Perfect Tense
  3. Present Continuous Tense
  4. Present Perfect ContinuousTense

Simple Present Tense

This Tense is generally describing as Habitual action, Daily activities, and Universal truths

Structures of Simple Present Tense

subject + v1- es or s

subject + do/does + not + v1

who/where/when + do/does + sub + v1

who + v1- s/es

Example Sentences of Simples Present Tense

  1. I drink coffee every morning and evening.
  2. I speak to him.
  3. I like her dress.
  4. The river flows towards the sea.
  5. I hear his voice in the Airport.
  6. Sun rises in the East.
  7. He doesn't play well.
  8. I do not take alcohol.
  9. This system doesn't support latest features.
  10. Where do you live?
  11. What do you do?
  12. Who makes this?

Present Perfect Tense

When an action takes place somewhere at a particular time is said to be Present Perfect Tense.

Structures of Present Perfect Tense

subject + has/have + v3 + object

subject + have/has + not + v3 + object

have/has + subject + v3 + object + ?

Example Sentences of Present Perfect Tense

  1. He has his own credit card.
  2. I have two pens.
  3. I have done my job.
  4. I have spoken to him.
  5. You have talk to him.
  6. I have not mobile.
  7. Have you read this?

Present Continuous Tense

When an action going on at the time of speaking.

Structures of Present Continuous Tense

subject + am/is/are + v-ing

subject + am/is/are (not) + v-ing

am/is/are + subject + v-ing

Example Sentences Of Present Continuous

  1. I am speaking to him.
  2. I am protecting him.
  3. He is waiting for you.
  4. They are playing cricket.
  5. Diana is not doing his work.
  6. Are you watching film tonight?
  7. Is your system working properly?

Present Perfect Continuous Tense

When an action is being at same time in the past and still continuing then it is said to be Present Perfect Continuous Tense.

Structures of Present PerfectContinuous Tense

subject + has/have + been + v-ing

subject + has/have + not + been + v-ing

have/has + subject + been + v-ing

Example Sentences of Present Perfect Continuous Tense

  1. I have been waiting for you.
  2. He has been working on this internship for a week
  3. Have you been looking for this mobile?
  4. she has not been speaking to him for three months.
  5. She has been sleeping for 3 hours.
  6. I have been caring for her.
  7. I haven't been cooking this dish for an hour.
