Verb

A verb is a word that denotes the action, state, of a person or thing in the sentence. . It generally tells us

  1. He walked a long distance (action)
  2. She is sitting on the chair (state)
  3. There is a glitch in this mobile (bein)
  1. What a thing or person does
    1. He plays violin
    2. She is writing
    3. They are running

Verb example sentence

  1. Ram is eating a mango.
  2. I have learned a new course.
  3. We are conducting a match on the ground.
  4. They are jogging in the park
  5. We are practicing for tomorrow's exam.

Transitive Verbs

A transitive Verb is a Verb that needs a direct object to complete the sentence. The object is a noun or pronoun.

  1. My sister cut vegetables for a meal.
  2. We change all things normally.
  3. He took advice from his mother.
  4. I wrote a letter to my friend.
  5. She always lifts heavy objects.

Intransitive Verbs

An tntransitive Verb does not need any direct object to complete the sentence.

Examples of Intransitive Verb

  1. The lion walks.
  2. The moon shines brightly.
  3. Fishes swim in the water.
  4. I looked up for the helicopter.
  5. I woke up early before the sun rises.
