Punctuation Marks

In any Language, we use some types of symbols while writing or reading. The word punctuation means Symbols. So these are some punctuation marks in English.

Full Stop (.)

A full stop is the most commonly used punctuation mark in the English language.

It represents Large Gaps or Pause

We use Full stop in....

  1. At the end of Imperative and Assertive Sentences.

    2. He gave me a pen yesterday.

    Close the window

  2. In the Abbreviation words

    3. U.S.A, U.A.E, U.N.O, etc.

  3. In Initials

    4. Dr.Strange, Mr. Beck, etc.

The Comma (,)

The Comma is a symbol that is used to separate the sequence of a word in a sentence.

Ex: John, Michelle, and Jane are the children of Martha and Jonas.

It indicates a small gap or pause

We use Comma in....

  1. To separate pair of wors in a series

    2. Ex: High and Low, Rich and Poor are not considered when you are here.

  2. To separate a series of Clause in a sentence

    3. Ex: If you help me, I will help you.

  3. At the end of reporting speech indirect speech

    4. Ex: He said to me,"I'm going to the mall now"

  4. After noun

    5. Mary, Is a student at Oxford

Question Mark (?)

It is used at the end of Interrogative Sentences or Question tags.

Example

  1. How are you?
  2. Where are you now?
  3. She is awesome, Isn't she?
  4. When did you come to pick me up?
  5. He plays football well, Isn't he?

Exclamation Mark (!)

An Exclamation mark is used to show emphasis. It is used in the middle of a sentence or at the end of a sentence.

The words Alas!, AH!, Hurrah!, Oh!, Wow! always end with Exclamatory Mark.

Example

  1. Stop! a child is crossing the road.
  2. Hurrah! I got a new phone.
  3. You look beautiful. Wow!

Hyphen

The hyphen is used in Compound words, to link words to prefixes, and also as a way to show word breaks.

Simply the hyphen indicates a higher gap, Lower than the Fullstop.

Example

  1. I work here part-time.
  2. My 9-year old-boy loves cricket.
  3. My mother-in-law supports me in every situation.

Apostrophe (')

An Apostrophe is used to express the possision of a noun and express the missing letters in a word (don't, I'll, etc.). It removes certain letters from the word.

Example

  1. He's mine.
  2. This is Raju's book
  3. I'll call you later
  4. He buys a phone as a gift for Paul's birthday.
  5. Katherine's hair is blond and curly.
