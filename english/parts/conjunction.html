Conjunction

  A Conjunction is a word used to connect clauses or sentences in the same clause.

You may assume it is like a bridge between two sides.

These are some words we used as Conjunctions- else, and, for, but, or, yet, after, although, as, etc.

Some Conjunctions are used in a couple like Both - and Either - or, Neither - nor, Whether - or, Not only - but also, etc. These are also called Correlative Conjunctions.

We have also compound conjunctions like As though, As well as, As soon as, As if, In order that, On condition that, Even if, so that, etc.

Example Sentences of Conjunction

  1. Damon and Stefan are Brothers.
  2. He came late to school, but her teacher excused him.
  3. I do not care whether you leave or stay.
  4. Either - buy it or leave it.
  5. We both love and care about her.
  6. We will come after you reached Mumbai.
  7. He bought a gift for his daughter.
  8. He saves some money in the bank so that he should not go into financial problems.
  9. Kara as well as Jonas was present at the party.
  10. He is taller than I am.
  11. He just came before you.

Note: "with" is not a conjunction but a preposition.
