Prefix

  A Prefix is a group of words or letters that are used to add at the beginning of the word. When we add a prefix the original word meaning gets changed.

For Example, Take the word Happy when we add a prefix (un) to this it changed to Unhappy means Not Happy. You can see that meaning of the original word becomes changes as the Opposite meaning of that

Prefix Meaning Examples
Non- (word), un- , im- , in- , il- , ir- not, opposite, against Unhappy, Illetarate, Unimportant, Nonsense, etc.
re- again, again Rematch, Revisit, Replay, Rearrangement, Reconstruct, etc.
mis- wrongly, not Misunderstand, Misfortune, Misconception, etc.
co- , com- , com- together Cooperate, Construct, Cooperation, Connection, Convey, Conclude, etc.
dis- , dif- , di- divide, apart Disease, Dissect, Discomfort, Disconnected, Different, Diffraction
