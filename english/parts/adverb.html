The Adverb

  An Adverb is a word that improves/modifies the meaning of the Verb or an Adjective.

Example

He walks slowly.

In the above example the word slowly modifies the verb walks

Example sentences of Adverb

  1. Pinky writes (verb) paragraphs clearly (adverb).
  2. This fruit is very (adverb) sweet (adjective).
  3. They play (verb) well(adverb).

Adverbs again classified into several kinds based on their meaning

Adverbs of Time

It defines when the action of the subject is executed.

Example sentences of Adverbs Of Time

  1. I have to listen to this before.
  2. Yesterday he and I arrived late at the college.
  3. He goes to the library daily.
  4. I have to eat this snack already.

Adverbs of Quantity or Degree

It shows how much or in what quantity or to what area.

Example sentences for Adverbs of Quantity

  1. He is so intelligent.
  2. Ramu's land is very large.
  3. I am fully eaten.
  4. She speaks in English very accurately.
  5. She sings pretty well.
  6. He is strong as Stephen.

Adverbs of Place

It shows where something happens.

Example sentences of Adverbs of Place

  1. He was standing there.
  2. Everywhere I go I see his face.
  3. He looked down.
  4. He can come in without anyone's permission.
  5. She comes inside my house.

Adverbs of Manner

It tells in what manner or how the action is done.

Example sentences of Adverbs of Manner

  1. This article is well written.
  2. The girl works hard to achieve her goals.
  3. He runs fastly.
  4. He always does his work quickly.
  5. I like to do work accurately.
