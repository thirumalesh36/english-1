Prepositions with examples

  Prepositions are such words placed before a noun or pronoun to show the relation between the person or thing denoted by it stands concerning something else

The Preposition joins a Noun to Verb

=> The Preposition joins a Noun to another noun

=> There are so many prepositions in English these are some of them.

Prepositions With Examples

    1. By :

    It is commonly used in the passive form of sentences and to express the manner of transport.

  1. The car was hitten by another car.
  2. I came to college on foot.
  3. She traveled by bus

    2. On :

    On is used to show the state of a thing or person.

    It is also used before days and dates.

  1. She is on the bike.
  2. He was born on March 28.
  3. There are high chances of a held meeting on Saturday.

    3. With :

    This preposition is used to accompanied by another person or thing

  1. He is going out with his cousin.
  2. He came to college with his Mother.
  3. She is safe when she is with him.

4. To :

    It is used to the ending point of time or place.

  1. He is going to NYC.
  2. I went to the airport.
  3. The college runs from 8 am to 3 pm

    5. Till

    It is used when the ending point is given.

  1. The office works till 6 pm

    6. From :

    It is used to specify a starting point of place or time.

  1. The LA is far from here.
  2. He comes from Indiana.

Compound Prepositions with Examples

    1. Above :

    It is used to specify the state of a thing or person.

  1. The Aeroplane flying above the sky

    2. Across :

    It is used to define a place or thing from one side to the other.

  1. They lived across the USA
  2. The girl walks across the road.

    3. Among :

    It is used when something is more than two.

  1. She selects for his job among all persons.

    4. Between :

    It is used when something is in between two

  1. He sits between us.
  2. He makes a clash between us.

    5. Beside

    It is used to give meaning to the next to or the side of

  1. I sit beside my friend in the theatre.
  2. He sat beside me in the front auditorium.

    6. Besides :

    It is used to give meaning of in addition to.

  1. She play football besides cricket.
  2. He speaks french French besides Tamil.

    7. Beneath :

    It is used to specify something at a lower level or layer.

  1. There are several elements beneath Mars.

    8. Within :

    We used this preposition to represent something inside or when we have to mention the maximum time.

  1. I shall complete this Internship within a week.
  2. He lived half of the century within the caves.
