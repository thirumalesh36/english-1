Future Tense And Its Types

When an action has not yet happened and does not exist then it is said to be Future Tense.

There are four types of Future Tense

  1. Simple Future Tense
  2. Future Perfect Tense
  3. Future Continuous Tense
  4. Future Perfect ContinuousTense

Simple Future Tense

This Tense generally tells us what will happen after that present action or event.

Structures of Simple Present Tense

subject +shall + v

subject + will not or won't + v1

will + subject + v?

Example Sentences of Simples Future Tense

  1. I will go tomorrow.
  2. I will not go tomorrow.
  3. Will you go tomorrow?
  4. They shall play tomorrow for their college.
  5. I won't take alcohol anymore.
  6. I shall write a letter to her.
  7. I shall speak to him.

Future Perfect Tense

When an action that expected to complete in the future then it is said to be Future Perfect Tense.

Structures of Future Perfect Tense

subject + will + have + v3

subject + will not or won't + have + v3

will + subject + have + v3 + ?

Example Sentences of Future Perfect Tense

  1. I will have spoken to her.
  2. I will not have spoken to her.
  3. Will he have spoken to her?
  4. I shall have written my exam by then.
  5. She will have left before you come to see her.
  6. I will have finished my work by lunchtime.
  7. Diana will have gone to Richmond by this time next month.

Future Continuous Tense

When an action will occur in the future and continue for an excepted length.

Structures of Future Continuous Tense

subject + will + be + v-ing

subject + will not or won't + v-ing

will + subject + be + v-ing

Example Sentences Of Future Continuous

  1. I will be protecting her.
  2. I shall be speaking to him.
  3. I won't be speaking to him.
  4. Will they be playing a game tomorrow?
  5. They will be singing songs tomorrow.
  6. He will be coming to visit the palace for three days.
  7. You will be in the USA by this time next week.

Future Perfect Continuous Tense

When an action that will continue up until a point in the future then it is said to be Future Perfect Continuous Tense.

Structures of Future Perfect Continuous Tense

subject + will + have + been + v-ing

subject + will not or won't + have + been + v-ing

will + subject + have + been + v-ing?

Example Sentences of Future Perfect Continuous Tense

  1. I shall have been speaking to her.
  2. I will have been exercising for 9 days.
  3. By 2023 I will have been living in Norway.
  4. Will you have been exercising for 9 days?
  5. I won't have been exercising for 9 days.
  6. I shall have been protecting her.
  7. They will have been playing cricket.
