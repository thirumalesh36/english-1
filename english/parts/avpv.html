Active Voice

When the subject of a sentence is a verb then the sentence is in the active voice. Here the subject is the doer of the action.

Passive Voice

When the subject of the sentence is acted on by the verb then it is in the passive voice. Here something is done to the subject.

Example

  1. Sarah eats a Banana. (active voice)
  2. A banana is eaten by Sarah (passive voice)

In the first sentence sarah is the subject and she does something so it is in the active voice. Here eats is the verb

Whereas in the second sentence something is done to Sarah means a banana is eaten by Sarah so it is in the passive voice. Here eaten is the verb.

Example Sentences for Active Voice and Passive Voice

  • 1. Active: Ramu collects money for charity.
  • Passive: Money is collected by Ramu for charity.
  • 2. Active: Close the window.
  • Passive: Let the window be closed.
  • 3. Active: She sings a song.
  • Passive: A song is sung by her.
  • 4. Active: I wrote an article.
  • Passive: An article was written by me.
  • 5. Active: The teacher teaches English.
  • Passive: English is taught by the teacher.
  • 6. Active: I am writing an essay.
  • Passive: An essay was being written by me.
  • 7. Active: Finish the work before you go.
  • Passive: Let the work finished before you go.
