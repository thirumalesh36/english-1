Pronouns

A pronoun is a word that is used instead of using a noun to represent something. By using pronouns we avoid the repetition of the noun.

Example

He (used to represent men without mentioning his name), She (used to represent women without mentioning her name), His, Him, Her, They, Them, You, We, etc.

Again pronoun is classified into 7 types

    1. Personal Pronouns

    Personal pronouns are pronouns used instead of a proper name of a person.

  1. Singular: I, my, mine, me, you, your, yours, she, her, hers, he, him, his
  2. Plural: We, our, ours, us, they, their, theirs, them

    2. Reflexive Pronouns

    These pronouns come when self is added to singular pronouns like him, her, my, your,it,and selves are added to plural pronouns like our, your, them,

    Example:

  1. I like myself.
  2. We love ourselves.
  3. Tell me about yourself.

    3. Relative Pronouns

    It is a pronoun that refers to some noun before going (Antecedent).

  1. who, which, whose, whom, that, etc.
  2. I met him who is our old friend.
  3. I go back to there where I had lost my keys.
  4. This is the thing that I told you about yesterday.
  5. He who always smiles.
  6. she is a woman whose face is glowing like the moon.

    4. Possessive Pronouns

    Possessive Pronouns are pronouns that only belong to their particular person (noun).

  1. mine, his, her, yours, ours, etc.

    5. Distributive Pronouns

    These are the pronouns when there are more than one persons or things to refer to them separately.

  1. either, neither, each, every, any, etc.

    6. Demonstrative Pronouns

    These are the pronouns used to indicate a particular thing or person.

  1. this, that, these, those, there, here, etc.

    7. Interrogative Pronouns

    These are similar to Relative pronouns but these are used to ask a question

  1. who, whose, whom, which, whoever, etc.
  2. Who lived here?
  3. Whose car is this?
  4. Which person did you see yesterday?
