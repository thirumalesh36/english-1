Idioms

1. A blessing in disguise.

Meaning: A good thing seems bad at first.

Example

Our first meeting was a Blessing in disguise

2. Water under the bridge

Meaning: Used when something from the past is not so important

Example

The way you dealt with your friend is Water under the bridge. Just tell him a sorry and be like good friends

3. Fit as a fiddle

Meaning: Being in good health

Example

The old person is 75 years old but he's as Fit as a fiddle.

4. Piece of cake

Meaning: very simple to deal with something to do

Example

The exam paper was Piece of cake for me to answer.

5. Let the cat out of the bag

Meaning: To tell someone about a secret.

Example

We are planning a party tonight for jane's birthday but don't let the cat out of the bag.

6. Hit the nail on the head

Meaning: To represent something perfect.

Example

I think you hit the nail on the head when you said that we are stepping into success.

7. You can't judge a book by its cover.

Meaning: We can't tell what someone is like from their appearance

Example

He seems like a very bad person, But he is so nice. You can't tell judge by its cover.

8. Don't bite off more than you can chew

Meaning: Don't try to do more than you can

Example

I think he has bitten off more than he can chew by doing all projects in a day.

9. You scratch my back and I'll scratch yours

Meaning: Used to tell If they do a favor and you'll help them in return

Example

He had an attitude that "You scratch my back and I'll scratch yours".

10. Add insult to injury

Meaning: To make a bad situation terrible by doing something else bad

Example

He not only went cinema without taking me but to add insult to injury took my car with him instead of his bike.

11. Once in a blue moon

Meaning: Something that happens in rare

Example

His film is released like Once in a blue moon.

12. The last straw

Meaning: The last problem in a sequence of problems

Example

It had been a hard day, so when the mobile phone was damaged, it was the last straw.

13. Cut the corners

Meaning: To make things easier

Example

To study all the subjects in a night I want to cut the corners by reading only important topics.

14. See eye to eye

Meaning: To represent the mutual agreement of two persons or to say two persons see things the same way

Example

Sometimes we argue on something, but I know we see eye to eye on most things.

15. Kill two birds with one shot

Meaning: To accomplish multiple tasks at the same

Example

While on my school trip I attend the museum and I met my relatives there it's like, Killing two birds with one stone.

16. Pinch Of Salt

Meaning: Do not take things very seriously if you don't believe in that

Example

Take her words with a pinch of salt, cause she tells things by exaggerating.

17. Sit on the fence

Meaning: Used when not taking any stand if two disputed situations happened to someone

Example

I'm like Sitting on the fence when my best friends fighting

18. The best of both worlds

Meaning: To take all the leads in two situations

Example

I have the best of both worlds, having nice company and a family.

19. Pull the wool over someone's eyes

Meaning: To hide the truth from someone

Example

It's so hard to pull the wool over his eyes cause he's an intelligent

20. Break a leg

Meaning: Wishing good luck to someone

Example

My family told me tobreak a leg before I am writing for exams.

21. Speak Of the Devil

Meaning: When someone appeared unexpectedly

Example

Oh boy! finally, you are here to speak of the devil

22. A lot on a plate

Meaning: When some have a lot of work to do and are busy

Example

I can't do this now, I have got a lot on a plate today.

23. Couch potato

Meaning: When someone is being lazy even if they had a lot of things to do

Example

He is playing video games instead of doing his homework he's like a couch potato.

24. Between the devil and the deep sea

Meaning: When you're captured between two undesirable situations

Example

If you support your daughter, your son will be hurt and vice versa. You're captured between the devil and the deep sea

25. Left out in the cold

Meaning: When you're ignored by someone

Example

I was left out in the cold in my company project.

26. Head in the clouds

Meaning: You're not in touch with the realities

Example

Many students have their heads in the clouds.

27. Burn your boat

Meaning: When you do something that makes it impossible to back to the earlier position

Example

I have burnt my boat with my colleague by criticizing him publicly.

28. Spill the beans

Meaning: Reveal secret information unintentionally

Example

I can't Spill the beans by telling our project work.

29. Better late than never

Meaning: To say someone in a situation that better to come late than not to come at all

Example

You came to college late, That's okay, better late than never.

30. Rule of thumb

Meaning: A proper and accurate guide based on their experience.

Example

As a rule of thumb, I do not start a new project during college time.
